The Sisters of Mercy Research and Redesign

CASE STUDY

Introduction

Redesigned the donation experience for The Sisters of Mercy to increase users, reduce friction, and create a more personalized experience.

My Role: UX/UI Researcher and Designer

Timeline: 10 weeks

Tools: Figma, Figjam, Google forms, Adobe Illustrator

Challenges

  • Understanding the diverse needs of users across different age groups
  • Implimenting a seamless experience for both new and returning donors
  • Addressing technical constraints while implementing design solutions

Design Goal: Create a clear, intuitive and trustworthy donation flow.

Process

  • Semi Structured Interviews that were conducted last quarter, but reanalyzed for this project
  • Affinity Mapping
  • Card Sorting
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • User Personas

Findings

  • Users didn't understand website navigation
  • Website experience didn't feel personalized when donating
  • Users were unsure about the impact of their donations
  • Lack of errors and Confirmation Messages

Design Process

1. Wireframes 2. Lo-Fidelity Prototype 3. Mid-Fidelity Prototype 4. High-Fidelity Prototype

Testing and Improvements

Tested with a college aged student and a recurring donor

    Improvements
  • Added a progress bar to show how close donation was to its goal
  • Made the donation form more clickable so users could fully interact
